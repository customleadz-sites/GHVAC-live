Is It Time?

Signs Your AC System Needs
to Be Replaced

Sometimes repair isn't the answer. Here's how to know when a new system makes more sense.

System Is 10+ Years Old

The average AC lasts 10–15 years. Once you hit that window, repairs become more frequent and a new system starts making financial sense.

Energy Bills Keep Climbing

Aging systems lose efficiency every year. A new high-efficiency unit can cut cooling costs by 20–40% compared to an older system.

Repairs Are Getting Expensive

If a repair costs more than 50% of a new system's price, replacement is the smarter investment. We'll always give you an honest answer.

Can't Keep Up in Summer Heat

If your system runs all day and still can't reach your thermostat setting, it's undersized or worn out. A properly sized new system changes everything.

Uses R-22 Refrigerant

R-22 (Freon) is no longer manufactured and has become very expensive. If your older system uses it, a new system will cost less to maintain long-term.

Uneven Temps Room to Room

Some rooms too hot, others too cold? This often points to an aging or improperly sized system. A new install with proper sizing fixes this for good.
